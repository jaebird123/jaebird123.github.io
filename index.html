Research

Biology is complicated. I will try to explain what I do in simple terms.

Tell your grandma what I do

I research cancer. Cancer happens when part of your body decides it doesn't want to cooperate with the rest. Cancer doesn't stop growing even when your other body parts tell it to stop. I try to figure out how quickly your cancer is growing and how to make it stop growing.

Tell your mom what I do

Your body is made of 37 trillion cells. You were originally one cell. Each cell makes new ones when it divides into two. We try to find the ancestors of each cell to make a family tree called a phylogeny. It is my job to make phylogenies for tumor cells. We use this to characterize different tumors.

Tell you what I do

In Professor Russell Schwartz's lab, I write code to help diagnose cancer. We detect the severity of a tumor by first "unmixing" a bulk tumor sample into subclones (different cell types), building a phylogeny (or tree of life) for the tumor, and finally using features from phylogentic features for each patient to predict the severity of the cancer.
